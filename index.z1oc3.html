TUT:Configuring snmptrapd

Snmptrapd is a Net-SNMP application that lets operators receive SNMP notifications ( informs and traps) and process them. By default, snmptrapd does not do anything with received notifications. You have to tell it what you want to receive notifications. To receive SNMPv1 or SNMPv2c notifications you need to tell snmptrapd your community strings.

Snmptrapd

The snmptrapd application typically listens to port 162 (and maybe 10162) and logs or acts upon any notifications ( TRAPs or INFORMs) that it receives. snmptrapd is configured through its snmptrapd.conf file. snmptrapd, at a minimum, needs access control settings to allow notifications using various SNMP protocols access to its logging and execution framework.
